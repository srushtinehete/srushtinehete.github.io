The subways of New York City are its lifeline. Pedestrian traffic to and from the Subway stations is extremely dense and experiences a high-paced condition, and ultimately stress.

To bring about a respite in these high-stress zones, we propose to create pause points of various sizes, shapes and functions along the pavements, around the busiest subway stops in Manhattan. The subway stops are devoid of natural light, and using the pavements or the medians to carve out light shafts, can greatly improve the mental health of the users.
